Throughout the course of the semester, my perspective on using AI in programming became more intentional. At the beginning of the semester, I mainly used AI to generate full solutions, often without fully understanding how the code worked. Over time, I shifted to using AI for specific tasks, like rewriting code for efficiency, improving the structure and naming conventions of my code base, and debugging. In this class, specifically, I used AI to improve code to be more object-oriented, to generate JUnit test cases, and to understand different concepts and issues with my code. Learning from Professor Woolfolk's industry experience and hearing from peers outside of class helped me to understand how AI is used in practice. As computer science students, it is important to keep up with the ever-changing industry. During this semester, I learned techniques like "vibe coding", where I can guide AI to make small changes rather than relying on it for complete solutions. This has helped me to complete assignments and projects more efficiently while still reinforcing the material. 

In the future, I plan to use AI in a more intentional and structured way. I will first break down problems and attempt solutions on my own, then use AI to identify gaps in my understanding and assist with targeted tasks rather than relying on it as a replacement for problem-solving. This approach is especially important in my research work, where independent thinking is critical. AI does not inherently know the correct answers or the best way to break down problems, so I will rely on my own reasoning to guide the direction of my work and use AI more as a support tool. Overall, this course has shown me how to integrate AI into my workflow in a way that improves both efficiency and understanding while still maintaining strong problem-solving skills.
